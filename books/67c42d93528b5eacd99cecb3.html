Didactica Magna

de John Amos Comenius

Vezi detalii
The Great Didactic or Didactica Magna, written by John Amos Comenius between 1627 and 1638 and first published in 1657, is considered one of the first major works on pedagogy. It outlines a universal method of teaching and education, emphasizing pansophism — the aim to teach all things to all people comprehensively. Comenius proposed a system of schools resembling the modern educational system, arguing for an inclusive education that reaches all levels of society.
