Overdoing Democracy: Why We Must Put Politics in its Place

de Robert B. Talisse

Vezi detalii
We live in an age of political polarization. As political beliefs on the left and the right have been pulled closer to the extremes, so have our social environments: we seldom interact with those with whom we don't see eye to eye. In this book, Talisse argues that when we overdo democracy, we allow it to undermine and crowd out many of the most important social goods that democracy is meant to deliver. What's more, in overdoing democracy, we spoil certain social goods that democracy needs in order to flourish. A thriving democracy needs citizens to reserve space in their social lives for activities not structured by political allegiances. To ensure the health and future of democracy, we need to forge civic friendships by working together in social contexts where political affiliations are not merely suppressed, but utterly beside the point.
