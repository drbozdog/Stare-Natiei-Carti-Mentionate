The Tipping Point: How Little Things Can Make a Big Difference

de Malcolm Gladwell

Vezi detalii
The Tipping Point: How Little Things Can Make a Big Difference is a debut book by Malcolm Gladwell, first published by Little, Brown in 2000. It seeks to explain and describe the mysterious sociological changes that mark everyday life. Gladwell defines a tipping point as 'the moment of critical mass, the threshold, the boiling point.' He explains how ideas, products, and behaviors spread in society, using examples such as the popularity surge in Hush Puppies shoes and the crime rate drop in New York City.
