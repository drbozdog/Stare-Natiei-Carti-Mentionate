Contractul social

Vezi detalii
The Social Contract, originally published as On the Social Contract; or, Principles of Political Right, is a 1762 French-language book by the Genevan philosopher Jean-Jacques Rousseau. The work theorizes about establishing legitimate authority in a political community, emphasizing the importance of a social contract that respects individual freedom yet maintains societal order. Rousseau argues that only the general will of the people has the right to legislate, aiming to create legislation that applies equally to all citizens.
