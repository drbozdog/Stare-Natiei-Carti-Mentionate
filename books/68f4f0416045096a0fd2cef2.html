Remembering the Neoliberal Turn: Economic Change and Collective Memory in Eastern Europe after 1989

de Edited by Veronika Pehe and Joanna Wawrzyniak

Vezi detalii
This book discusses how societies, groups, and individuals remember and make sense of global neoliberal change in Eastern Europe. It examines processes such as deindustrialization, privatization, and abrupt social reorganization during the 1990s and their collective memory across society today, contributing to current political climates and identities.
