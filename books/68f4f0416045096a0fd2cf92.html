The New Testament is a collection of 27 Christian texts written in Koine Greek, forming the second major division of the Christian Bible. It includes the teachings and person of Jesus, four gospels, the Acts of the Apostles, various epistles attributed to Paul and other apostles, and the Book of Revelation. The canon developed gradually over the first few centuries of Christianity.
