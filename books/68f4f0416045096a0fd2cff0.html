Rhinoceros

de Eugène Ionesco

Vezi detalii
Rhinoceros is a play by Eugene Ionesco, written in 1959. It explores the themes of conformity, culture, fascism, responsibility, logic, mass movements, mob mentality, philosophy, and morality. The story is about the inhabitants of a small French town who turn into rhinoceroses. The central character, Bérenger, remains the only human not to succumb to this metamorphosis. The play criticizes the rise of fascism and Nazism and highlights the absurdity of mass conformity.
