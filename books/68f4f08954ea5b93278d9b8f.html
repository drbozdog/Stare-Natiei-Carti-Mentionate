The Quiet Before: On the Unexpected Origins of Radical Ideas

de Gal Beckerman

Vezi detalii
An elegantly argued and exuberantly narrated look at the building of social movements—from the 1600s to the present—and how current technology is undermining them. It explores how revolutions don’t just happen noiseily on the streets, but are often fueled by quieter intellectual exchanges in secluded spaces. The book connects personal narratives and historical events to the mechanisms of social change, warning how social media is impacting our methodologies for organizing and activism.
