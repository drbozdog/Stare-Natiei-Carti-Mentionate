Prisoners of Geography: Ten Maps That Tell You Everything You Need to Know About Global Politics

de Tim Marshall

Vezi detalii
Prisoners of Geography is a non-fiction book that examines geopolitics through geography. It challenges the belief that technology has overridden the limitations geography imposes on geopolitics. By focusing on various global regions such as Russia, China, the US, and others, the book delves into how geography influences international affairs, explaining events like Russia's annexation of Crimea. Through examples, it portrays geography as a critical factor in the historic and present political landscape.
