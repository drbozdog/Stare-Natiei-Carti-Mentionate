A Theory of Justice

de John Rawls

Vezi detalii
A Theory of Justice is a 1971 work of political philosophy and ethics by philosopher John Rawls in which he attempts to provide a moral theory alternative to utilitarianism and addresses the problem of distributive justice. The theory employs an updated form of Kantian philosophy and a variant of social contract theory, introducing concepts such as the 'original position' and the 'veil of ignorance'.
